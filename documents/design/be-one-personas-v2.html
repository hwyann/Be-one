Be-one — Personas v2 Design Review

Prepared for Jess · 2026-07-18 · this artifact proposes the multi-user + persona-differentiated UX for the post-hackathon phase.

What's new vs. today:

  • Login accepts any email (removes the single-user restriction)
  • On first login, capture display name + role (IC or Exec)
  • Map view stays universal; My Thread differs per role
  • Exec map adds an alignment health strip and gives the AI summary top billing
  • Exec My Thread swaps to a Team Overview if they have direct reports (or a lightweight "my own goals" list if they don't)

New 1. Login v2 — open sign-in + first-time profile capture

Two flows in one screen. Existing users just enter their email. First-time users see the profile step after clicking the magic link.

Design decisions needing your confirmation:
  • Roles are just two options for MVP: IC and Exec. Team Lead lives under Exec. Room to add later.
  • Role affects which My Thread rendering they see; both roles see the same Map view.
  • Role is user-editable via a settings link (deferred story) — not locked at signup.
  • Any email is allowed. If you want to gate at a domain level (e.g. `@bekindlabs.com` for now), that's a one-line variable — flag it.

What's Exec-specific here?
  • Alignment health strip at the top of Map view — 4 tiles: on-track / at-risk / behind / contributors-linked count for the current quarter
  • AI summary promoted to the card itself (not just inside History panel) — Exec's primary information need is "what's the state of this objective?"
  • All contributors' avatars visible on each KR (not just the viewer's own) — Exec needs to see who's on the hook
  • KR actions reduce to "View history" — Exec doesn't check in on other people's KRs
  • Exec can still open History → sees the full chronological check-in trail + summary

Design decisions needing your confirmation:
  • What defines a "direct report"? Simplest: any user whose individual-objective's `linked_company_objective_id` matches a company objective Hiroshi owns. Cleaner (later): a proper `reports_to` column on users. Start with the first.
  • If Hiroshi has personal individual objectives too, they render above the Team Overview grid in a smaller "My own goals" section (not shown here)
  • Clicking a report card drills into their My Thread as a read-only view
  • Alternative framing considered but rejected: a single flat list of every individual objective — too noisy for Execs with 5+ reports
